Frequently Asked Questions

Answers before you need them.

Will this door still glide smoothly after several years of daily use? +
Yes — and the design is built specifically to maintain that performance over time. The Full Lite Gliding Patio Door uses adjustable stainless steel rollers and track that can be recalibrated in the field, years after installation. If the home settles, the frame shifts slightly, or the operation becomes stiff over time, the rollers can be re-adjusted without replacing any hardware or calling a service technician. The capacity for adjustment is built into the door because MP Doors designed it to last the home — not just the first season.
Will the track get dirty and become hard to slide over time? +
The Full Lite Gliding uses a flush-glaze design, which means the glass sits level with the door frame rather than behind a raised outer profile. There is no ledge on the frame face to collect debris, and the track profile minimizes the recessed channel that typically traps grit and dust in conventional sliding door systems. Regular cleaning of the track with a dry cloth or soft brush will maintain performance. If the door develops resistance after years of use, the adjustable roller system is the first adjustment point — not a replacement.
How does the gliding panel lock, and is it secure? +
The gliding panel locks via a key-cylinder locking mechanism that engages into the door frame. The system also includes an anti-lift design so the sliding panel cannot be lifted out of the track from the exterior. If you notice the key is stiff or the lock requires more effort than expected in the early weeks after installation, contact MP Doors at 1-888-366-7717 — the most common cause is latch alignment that can be resolved without removing hardware. The locking mechanism carries a 10-year warranty under the Limited Lifetime Warranty program.
Is gliding operation or hinged operation better for my opening? +
The right choice depends on the available space around the opening, not just its size. A gliding door uses no interior floor clearance — the panel slides along a track within the door frame width, so furniture, rugs, and deck layouts directly adjacent to the opening are unaffected. A hinged door requires clear floor space equal to the panel width for the sweep arc — typically 36 to 72 inches of unobstructed clearance depending on configuration. For openings where floor or deck space is limited, the Full Lite Gliding is the better fit. For buyers who prefer traditional French-door operation or have the clearance for a panel sweep, the Hinged Full Lite is the comparable option. Both use the same composite construction engineered with HydroShield Technology and deliver the same full-glass view.
How does this door compare to the Shaker Gliding model? +
Both use the same composite construction and HydroShield Technology. The Full Lite Gliding is a flush-glaze design optimized for maximum glass area and a clean, modern profile. The Shaker Gliding has a Shaker-style rail and stile pattern, and is available with optional built-in blinds and a factory pet door on select configurations.
What does "full lite" mean — how much glass does this door actually have? +
Full lite means the panel is entirely glass — there are no composite or wood rails, stiles, or inserts dividing the glass area. The Full Lite Gliding delivers an unobstructed view from the interior to the exterior across the entire panel surface. Combined with flush-glaze construction — where the glass sits level with the frame face rather than recessed behind it — the result is a clean, contemporary sight line with maximum light transfer.
What is flush-glaze construction and why does it matter? +
Flush-glaze construction means the glass panel sits flush with the surface of the door frame rather than sitting behind a raised outer profile. The practical results are threefold: there is no raised ledge on the frame face to collect water or debris; the sight line from inside to outside is uninterrupted by frame shadow; and the overall appearance of the door — from both the interior and exterior — reads as intentional and contemporary. Conventional sliding doors typically have a recessed glass profile that creates both a visual and a maintenance difference.
What sizes are available for the Full Lite Gliding Patio Door? +
Standard configurations are 60 in. × 80 in. and 72 in. × 80 in. Custom dimensions beyond those sizes are available through The Home Depot Custom Door Program if your rough opening requires a non-standard fit. Available configurations include single sliding panel or double sliding panel formats, and the door can be specified with right-sliding or left-sliding operation depending on your opening layout.
Is the Full Lite Gliding Patio Door Energy Star certified? +
Yes — the Full Lite Gliding is Energy Star certified across all four U.S. climate zones: Northern, North Central, South Central, and Southern. The LoE glass with Argon fill manages heat transfer in both directions — keeping heat inside a Minnesota home in winter and outside a Houston home in summer — under the same certification. There is no regional variant of this door; the standard configuration qualifies in every continental U.S. climate.
How does composite construction hold up in coastal climates? +
Composite construction engineered with HydroShield Technology seals all six sides of the door system against moisture intrusion. Salt air, coastal humidity, and sustained UV exposure leave no mark on a material that has no moisture entry point. Wood doors fail in coastal environments because moisture enters the grain and begins rot and delamination; steel frames corrode under salt-air exposure. The composite frame has neither of those vulnerabilities, making it the appropriate material choice for Florida Gulf Coast, South Carolina Low Country, and Gulf of Mexico coastal installations where wood and steel frames are consistently problematic.
What does "engineered with HydroShield Technology" mean in practical terms? +
This door is engineered with HydroShield Technology, which seals every edge and surface of the door system — frame, panels, edging, and all six sides — against moisture intrusion. The practical outcome is that this door cannot rot, cannot delaminate at frame edges, and cannot warp from moisture cycling. There is no maintenance cycle associated with this door because there is no moisture-related degradation mechanism in the material. The sealing applies to the entire door system, not just the exterior face.
What is covered under the warranty? +
The Full Lite Gliding carries a Limited Lifetime Warranty on structural components — frame, panels, and astragals — for as long as the original purchaser owns the home. The locking mechanism is warranted for 10 years. The glass seal carries a 20-year prorated warranty. The factory painted finish carries a 2-year defect warranty. Labor is not covered under any warranty term, including installation, repair, and reinstallation. Warranty coverage requires professional installation by a licensed and bonded contractor and applies to the original purchaser only in an owner-occupied single-family home. The warranty is not transferable to a subsequent buyer.
What should I do if the door arrives damaged? +
Before the delivery crew leaves, open the packaging and inspect the frame, glass panels, and hardware. Photograph any damage before moving the door. Report delivery damage within 5 calendar days of receipt by calling 1-888-366-7717 or emailing customerservice@mpdoors.com. Delivery damage reported after the 5-day window is not eligible for coverage — that deadline does not extend regardless of circumstances. Contact MP Doors directly; you will not be asked to return the door to a retail location.
Does the Full Lite Gliding come with a screen door? +
A screen is not included in the standard package. Compatible screen systems designed for this door's frame dimensions are available separately through The Home Depot. Confirm the correct compatible screen model with a store associate at the time of purchase — do not assume a screen from a previous sliding door installation will fit.
Can I see this door in a store before buying, or is it online only? +
The MasterPiece Series Full Lite Gliding Patio Door is available through The Home Depot both in-store and online. The Home Depot is the exclusive retail outlet for this product — it is not sold through independent dealers or other retail channels. If your local store does not have the specific configuration displayed, an associate can assist with an online order with delivery.
